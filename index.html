About Me

I'm Ritwika — an AI researcher working at the intersection of neural optimization, combinatorial reasoning, and large-scale logistics systems.

I completed my B.Tech in Computer Science from IIT Madras and my MS in Computer Science from Purdue University. I also worked as an SDE at Amazon (SCOT), where I developed a deep interest in routing, operations research, and middle-mile logistics.

My current research focuses on building Routing Foundation Models (RFM) — neural architectures that:

  • approximate MILP solvers (MILP-Transformer),
  • generate feasible routing priors (Diffusion Warm-Starts),
  • predict network dynamics (Mamba World Models),
  • encode facility-constraint interactions (Constraint Graph Transformers),
  • and enable real-time re-optimization (Proximal Refinement Networks).

This work is geared toward Amazon-scale routing, planning, and simulation.
