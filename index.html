Routing Foundation Model (RFM)

Routing Foundation Model (RFM)

Technical Monograph

RFM is a unified neural optimization architecture designed for Amazon-scale routing, logistics planning, and mixed-integer optimization. It integrates five specialized modules into a single differentiable system that approximates MILP reasoning while enabling real-time routing, forecasting, and scenario planning.

Abstract

Large-scale routing networks—such as middle-mile, last-mile, and multi-modal Amazon logistics systems—rely on massive mixed-integer linear programs (MILPs) that are computationally expensive to solve repeatedly under operational deadlines. The Routing Foundation Model (RFM) treats routing optimization as a learnable distributional problem, amortizing computation across network instances. The monograph introduces a unified neural architecture that embeds MILP structure, generates feasible routing priors, forecasts network evolution, and refines decisions through optimization-like steps. The goal is real-time, near-feasible routing decisions with strong generalization across topologies and dynamic operating conditions.

RFM Architecture

The Routing Foundation Model consists of five tightly coupled modules, each addressing a specific dimension of routing and MILP reasoning.

1. MILP-Aware Encoder

The encoder represents routing MILPs directly from their algebraic structure. Variables, constraints, facility types, and graph connectivity are embedded into a bipartite constraint–variable graph. This preserves flow balance, timing rules, capacity bounds, and network topology. The encoder provides a structured representation over which all downstream components operate.

2. MILP-Transformer (Neural Surrogate Solver)

The core solver approximates iterative MILP optimization through transformer layers. Each layer incorporates:

  • constraint violation signals derived from Ax - b,
  • dual-inspired messages pushing solutions toward feasibility,
  • Mixture-of-Experts specializing in flow, capacity, timing, and routing constraints,
  • latent refinement updates similar to proximal or descent steps.

The MILP-Transformer functions as a learned primal–dual procedure, producing near-feasible, high-quality routing assignments without branch-and-bound.

3. Neural Routing Optimization Model (NROM)

NROM provides global reasoning before refinement. It interprets routing problems through graph attention and learns to produce strong initial assignments—covering route selection, facility interactions, and path feasibility. NROM acts as a proposal generator feeding into the MILP-Transformer.

4. Diffusion Routing Prior

The diffusion model learns the high-dimensional geometry of feasible routing structures. Its denoising process enforces route consistency and structural feasibility. It is used to:

  • generate diverse warm-starts for large MILPs,
  • recover feasible structures under disruptions,
  • stabilize optimization during dynamic routing.

5. Routing World Model (SSM/Mamba)

A sequential model predicting the evolution of logistics networks over time, including:

  • congestion propagation,
  • arrival delays,
  • facility saturation,
  • SLA-risk evolution.

The world model enables multi-step planning through differentiable rollouts—similar to MuZero but specialized for routing.

Summary

Together, these five components form a full-stack neural optimization system capable of reasoning over constraints, generating plans, simulating future states, and refining solutions. RFM is designed for real-world deployment in Amazon-scale transportation networks, where latency, scale, and feasibility are critical.
