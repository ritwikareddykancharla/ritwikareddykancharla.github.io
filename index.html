About

I work at the intersection of neural optimization, routing, and large-scale logistics. My current research focuses on transformer-based surrogate solvers for mixed-integer linear programs (MILPs), diffusion-based routing priors, constraint graph architectures, and world models for Amazon-scale middle-mile routing.

Research Interests

Neural Optimization Transformers Routing & Logistics World Models Diffusion Models MILP Surrogates Combinatorial Reasoning Operations Research

Papers & Projects

Routing Foundation Model (RFM)

A long-term research program on surrogate MILP solvers, diffusion routing priors, world models, and constraint graph architectures for large logistics networks.

MILP-Transformer: A Neural Surrogate Solver for Routing MILPs

A transformer-based surrogate solver embedding MILP algebra, constraint violations, and dual signals. ICML-style architecture with refinement steps.

Diffusion Warm-Start Models for Routing

Diffusion models generating feasible warm-starts for large routing MILPs to accelerate solver latency.

Mamba World Models for SLA & Congestion Forecasting

Sequential world models for predicting congestion, SLA slack, and network propagation at Amazon middle-mile scale.

Constraint Graph Transformers

Graph architectures modeling MILP constraint–variable interactions across routing and facility networks.

Proximal Refinement Networks

Real-time re-optimization modules correcting routing decisions during disruptions without re-solving the MILP.
